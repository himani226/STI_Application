Grand Challenges

The Platform provides congenial environment to connect people, curate local challenges, co-develop ideas and facilitate innovative solutions. The platform has been conceptualized to offer dedicated feature to highlight challenges linked with the development priorities & sustainable development goals of the State as well as technological needs of the industry and enterprises, which have been mapped by holding interactive discussions with Administrative Departments and Industries/Industrial Associations. The initiative has also been taken to provide technology based solutions developed by Research Institutes, Universities, Innovators and Startups for addressing identified problems. In future, platform is envisaged to act networking platform to connect technology providers with technology seekers.

State Challenges

Agriculture Crop Diversification

  • Focused Research and Commercial Efforts on Fruits, Flowers, Vegetables, Oil seed for developing viable alternatives and their value addition.
  • Tools and Machinery for acceleration of the diversification -maize grain drier, cotton picker, sugarcane harvester, sugarcane trench planter, oilseed drill, vegetable picker, garlic planter, potato planter,required
  • Revival of Traditional Crops

Agri-residue Management

  • Standardization of improved machinery components for pre-processing of straw.
  • Scientific management of lechate from biomass storage sites.
  • Scientific management of ash generated from combustion of paddy straw based fuels.
  • Need to establish to refaction technology for paddy straw

Smart Agriculture

  • Tech Intervention for farm chemicals, disease surveillance, water & nutrient requirement
  • Zinc micro nutrient deficiency most widespread in Punjab
  • Nano based micro-nutrients and fertilisers
  • Precision Farming

Farm Mechanization

  • Refinement/Standardisation/Efficiency Improvement of Farm Equipment.
  • Lack of mechanisation in horticulture and vegetable crops.
  • Develop Digital Aggregators for renting farm machinery.
  • Drone-As-A-Service (DrAAS) in agriculture.

Food Processing

  • Linking agri production with agri-processing-inclusion of rural micro-processing units for primary processing and value addition.
  • Value addition of waste generated by f&v units
  • Alternate viable options for processing of damaged food grains/ under sized legumes & pulses /broken rice.
  • Analysis and management of aflatoxin in food products.
  • Smart and biodegradable packaging.
  • Block chain technology for supply chain management.

Animal Husbandry

  • Managing heat stress/ reproductive cycle.
  • State 60% deficient in green fodder crop (0.1 % against 5% area under fodder) - tech interventions required.
  • Precision dairy farming -sensor based monitoring.
  • Ai based early disease diagnosis (mastitis) .
  • Managing stray cattle - cost effective nutrition,climate resilient housing & breeding using advance techniques.
  • Technology for utilization of citrus peel /other food waste into cattle & poultry feed.
  • Valorisation of poultry waste.

Healthcare

  • Detection of pre-malignant & malignant lesions (cervical /breast)
  • Non-invasive tools –anemia, sugar,
  • Ai platform for screening of disorders in public health facilities
  • Pregnancy and newborn screening interventions
  • Old age health monitoring & care system

Air Pollution

  • Disposal of ash collected from agro based boilers. R&d need for utilization of fuel combustion by-products.
  • Technological interventions for air pollution control measures in plywood and textile & dyeing industrial sector.
  • Controlling indoor air pollution in centrally air-conditioned buildings.

Water pollution

  • Low cost village sullage water management system
  • Reviving of village ponds to recharge underground water
  • Develop low cost online monitoring system for monitoring river quality (ai based models for issuing of future alerts)
  • Develop & promote industrial technologies requiring less water
  • Reuse of treated waste water
  • Effective remediation options of waste water (sewage, dairy, textile etc.)

Solid Waste Management Challenges

  • Environmental optimization tools for waste collection systems using gis and its extensions
  • Technologies for landfill gas recovery
  • Innovative biodegradable materials to substitute multi-layered plastics
  • Safe disposal measures for batteries and domestic hazardous waste
  • Water-based dust suppression systems for c&d sites to minimize particulate mater

Energy

  • Technology for new sources of energy-compressed biogas, manufacturing
  • Low cost solar tracking system
  • Workable models for cleaning of solar panels
  • Introduce scientific processing and treatment of municipal, urban and industrial solid waste to target additional power generation
  • Network of e-charging stations

Industry Challenges

Bicycle Industry

Punjab houses 90% bicycle manufacturing industry of the country and produces 10.50 million bicycles annually

Technological up-gradation and R&D Interventions required

  • R&D for import substitution for bicycle industry parts for aluminum and carbon alloy frames, gear shift and disc brakes, twisted spokes and suspension.
  • Development of low cost indigenous components.

Forging cluster

Around 300 forging units operating in the state and forging of 105 million tons of alloy steel annually

Technological up-gradation and R&D Interventions required

  • Oil heating pumping filtration (hpf) unit.
  • Waste heat recovery system (whr).
  • Automation.
  • Refractory & insulation
  • Energy efficient burners

Textile & dyeing industry

Around 260 textile & dyeing industries operational in Ludhiana

Technological up-gradation and R&D Interventions required

  • Technical textiles.
  • R&d on indigenous development of specialty fibers.
  • High technology polymers for increased used in agro/medical/sports textile etc .
  • Effluent management

Brick Kilns

Around 3000 brick kilns producing 1800 crores solid clay bricks annually using coal as fuel for baking of bricks. Annual turnover of more than rs.7200 crores. Annual coal consumption of brick sector is 21.6 lakh tonnes (47.5 lakh tonnes equivalent co2 emissions) amounting to rs. 2160 crores. Consumption of 3.28 lakh cubic meters of top fertile soil annually

Technological up-gradation and R&D Interventions required

  • Setting up of common facility center for brick sector for carrying out r&d studies, clay testing laboratory, demonstration of state-of-art technology for brick manufacturing and firing.
  • Facilitation w.r.t. Setting up of resource efficient brick manufacturing units and related reb manufacturing machinery.
  • Support w.r.t. Machinery for clay brick extrusion, there being no machinery manufacturer in punjab and lack of technical support for importing the machinery.
  • Pneumatic fuel feeding system for existing high draft kiln technology which can reduce fuel consumption & improve brick quality.

Steel Re-rolling Cluster

Around 300 mills manufacturing ~ 40 lakh tons of steel bars consuming about 2222 gwh of energy annually

Technological interventions required

  • Design of png based re-heating furnace.
  • Energy efficiency in electrical systems.
  • High efficiency waste heat recovery system.

Foundry Cluster

Punjab is the hub of foundries with around 900 cupola units and 400 induction units operational in state. An energy intensive sector having lot of potential for energy and waste minimization

R&D Interventions required

  • Utilization of waste foundry sand
  • Exploring improvements in pattern materials for minimizing product rejection rate.
  • Png based cupola furnaces
  • Automation

Agriculture Implants

Highest per capita agriculture implements in India

Technological Interventions required

  • R&D on upgrading metallurgy of wearable parts so as to compete with international companies

Leather Industries

Technological Interventions required

  • Low cost technology for chromeless tanning
  • Chemical free dehairing operations & limited salt consumption/ preservation
  • Laser cutting machinery for Leather Industry
  • Computer aided design
  • Bio based leather
  • Effluent Management

Sports Goods Sector

Jalandhar: One of the largest manufacturers of sports goods in India.

Technological Interventions required

  • Nano-based solutions for an added advantage such as high strength and stiffness, durability, reduced weight, abrasion resistance, etc. in sporting equipment
  • Automation
  • Waste Minimization
